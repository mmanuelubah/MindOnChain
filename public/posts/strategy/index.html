Strategy

[!info]+ Important

MyEasyAgent is more than a business; it is a mission-driven PropTech platform aimed at bridging the gap between the “average Nigerian” and their right to a home. In a market like Lagos, where personal networks (“Who you know”) often outweigh financial capability (“What you pay”), MyEasyAgent prioritizes performance, trust, and transparency over deep pockets. This blueprint defines the What, Why, and How of our journey, providing a clear roadmap for execution.

1. Executive Summary

MyEasyAgent is a trust-centric PropTech ecosystem tailored specifically for the Nigerian real estate market. Unlike conventional listing sites that prioritize paid visibility, our platform leverages:

  • Performance-Based Ranking: Agents are ranked by delivery, not by payment.

  • Usage-Based Monetization: Users pay only when actively hunting for homes.

1.1 Problem Statement

The Nigerian real estate market suffers from:

  1. Fraudulent listings

  2. Exploitative agency fees

  3. Lack of verified property intelligence

1.2 Solution

Our solution integrates:

  • Regulated Commitment Fees: ₦2,500 per inspection to ensure accountability

  • Verified Property Intelligence: Physical and digital audits of properties via HomeView

1.3 Mission

To democratize access to affordable, verified housing in Nigeria through transparent, tech-enabled connections.

1.4 Vision

To establish MyEasyAgent as the primary trust infrastructure in Nigerian real estate, eliminating fear of exploitation and ensuring safe transactions for all users.

2. Business Model & Strategy

2.1 Revenue Model

Our revenue framework is designed for sustainability, alignment with user success, and scalability.

2.1.1 Unit Economics

  1. HuntSmart Pass (Primary Revenue Driver)

    • ₦15,000 usage-based pass

    • Includes 1 complimentary inspection

    • 4 additional prioritized inspection slots (subject to ₦2,500 commitment fee)

  2. Commitment Fees: ₦2,500 per inspection

    • Platform Share: ₦500 (operational overhead, verification)

    • Agent Share: ₦2,000 (incentivizes attendance, covers transport)

2.1.2 Commission Split (Performance Fees)

  1. Direct Deals: 50/50 split between agent and platform

  2. Sub-Direct Deals: 50/25/25 split (Direct Agent / Sub-Direct Agent / Platform)

2.1.3 Scalability Plan

  • Users only pay when actively hunting, avoiding subscription fatigue

  • Platform can scale without forcing long-term subscriptions

2.2 Market Positioning

MyEasyAgent is positioned as a Trust Infrastructure & Matchmaker, not just a listing site.

  1. Challenges with Traditional Sites

    • Platforms like PropertyPro.ng and Nigeria Property Centre operate on “Pay-to-Play” subscriptions.

    • Leads are generated but results and safety are not guaranteed.

  2. Core Positioning Statement

    “For the average Nigerian and hustling Lagos family tired of ghost listings and exploitative agency fees, MyEasyAgent guarantees verified property access through a performance-based connector.”

  3. Solving Transaction Leakage

    • Prevents offline deal skimming (“Sidelining”) using a Digital Handshake (QR Check-in)

    • Tracks deals from inspection to closing

4. Risk Assessment: The “Bad Sides”

Even with safeguards, these risks exist:

  1. Agent Resistance: 25–50% commission is significantly higher than 0% competitors charge

  2. Sidelining Vulnerability: Agents may try to hide deals to retain full commission

  3. Scaling Bottlenecks: Manual HomeView inspections are resource-intensive

  4. Market Speed vs. Process: Lagos rental deals move fast; legal reviews may be perceived as delays

  5. Legal Limits: Provisional Offer ≠ Tenancy Agreement; due diligence still required

  6. Upfront Fee Psychology: Users expect free browsing, overcoming this is critical

5. Strategic Solutions & Success Probabilities

5.1 Success Probability (Risk-Weighted)

  • Financial Sustainability: 70% (Revenue from HuntSmart Pass & Commitment Fees)

  • Trust & Adoption: 55% (Overcoming psychological barriers)

  • Sidelining Prevention: 40% (Requires strong digital “locks”)

5.2 Solution Set

  1. Leakage Prevention: Legal Review Lock warns clients that paying without verification voids platform protections

  2. Scaling via Graduated Trust: HomeView Badge for team-verified properties; incentivized video walkthroughs for agent-verified listings

  3. Remote Handover Script: Semi-Direct agents can avoid site travel while protecting commission splits

  4. Fast Market Fix: Instant Digital Receipts sent to agents/clients immediately upon “I Want This House” click

6. HomeView Audit & Checklist

[!danger] Do not launch without at least 20 HomeView-verified properties. Launching an empty platform undermines HuntSmart Pass value.

6.1 Professional Property Audit Checklist

A. Exterior & Structural

  • Inspect structural cracks, roof leaks, and drainage/flood history

  • Security: fence height, gate integrity, general access

B. Utilities & Services

  • Water: Pump test, tank availability, iron content

  • Electricity: Prepaid vs. estimated billing; socket testing

  • Waste Management: Verify PSP collection points

C. Interior Audit

  • Flooring: Check for hollow tiles, ventilation

  • Cabinetry: Termite/dampness inspection

  • Plumbing: Flush toilets; check soakaway proximity

D. Neighborhood Context

  • Road access during rainy season, noise levels, proximity to markets/churches/bars

  • Estimated commute times, mobile network coverage (MTN, Airtel, Starlink)

[!info] HomeView transforms house viewing into a professional Property Audit, central to the HuntSmart Pass value proposition.

10. Inspection Commitment Fee & Booking Management

Below is a clear, professional, and team-ready version of the inspection payment and cancellation logic. This document is designed for developers, testers, operations, and management to follow during implementation and quality assurance.

System Logic Specification

This document defines the official rules for handling:

  • Client no-shows

  • Cancellations

  • Refunds

  • Agent payments

  • Platform fees

  • Booking time states

  • Abuse prevention

All system behavior must follow this specification exactly.

1. Inspection Commitment Fee Structure

Total Fee Per Inspection

Each client pays:

₦2,500 per confirmed booking

Breakdown
Recipient Amount
Agent ₦2,000
Platform ₦500
Important Rule

Funds are not distributed immediately.
Distribution only happens after:

  • Inspection completion, OR

  • Client no-show, OR

  • Late cancellation (after lock period)

No early allocation is allowed.

2. Booking Time States

Every booking must always exist in one of these three states:

1. Scheduled (More than 24 Hours Remaining)

  • Time remaining: ＞ 24 hours

  • Booking is flexible

  • Cancellations allowed

2. Locked (Within 24 Hours)

  • Time remaining: ≤ 24 hours

  • System automatically locks booking

  • No manual override

3. Completed / Expired

  • Inspection finished OR

  • No-show confirmed

  • Record permanently locked

Locking occurs automatically exactly 24 hours before inspection time.

3. Client Cancellation Rules

A. Client Cancels Before 24 Hours (Not Locked)

Conditions

  • Time remaining ＞ 24 hours

  • Status = Scheduled

System Must:

  • Cancel the booking

  • Refund ₦2,500 to client

  • Restore 1 inspection credit

  • Remove ₦2,000 from agent expected earnings

  • Cancel ₦500 platform pending revenue

  • Log cancellation ID

  • Log refund ID

Status

“Cancelled — Refunded (Early)”

B. Client Cancels Within 24 Hours (Locked)

Conditions

  • Time remaining ≤ 24 hours

  • Status = Locked

System Must:

  • Deny refund

  • Do not restore credit

  • Forfeit ₦2,500

  • Mark as late cancellation

  • Pay agent ₦2,000

  • Pay platform ₦500

  • Log forfeiture

Status

“Locked — Late Cancel (No Refund)”

4. Agent Cancellation Rules

A. Agent Cancels Before 24 Hours
Conditions

  • Time remaining ＞ 24 hours

  • Not locked

System Must:

  • Cancel booking

  • Refund ₦2,500 to client

  • Restore client credit

  • Remove ₦2,000 agent earnings

  • Remove ₦500 platform allocation

  • Issue agent warning

  • Log incident

Status

“Cancelled by Agent — Refunded”

B. Agent Cancels Within 24 Hours (Locked)

Conditions
  • Time remaining ≤ 24 hours
System Rules
Default:
  • Cancellation is blocked
Emergency Override (Admin Approved):

  • Refund ₦2,500 to client

  • Agent receives ₦0

  • Platform receives ₦0

  • Issue serious warning

  • Escalate to admin review

Status

“Agent Cancelled After Lock — Penalty”

5. Client No-Show Rules

Trigger Conditions

A no-show is triggered when:

  • Inspection timer expires

  • Client did not confirm completion

  • Client did not cancel

  • Agent attended

System Must Automatically:

  • Mark client as “No Show”

  • Forfeit ₦2,500

  • Pay agent ₦2,000

  • Pay platform ₦500

  • Deduct 1 inspection credit

  • Lock record permanently

  • Log attendance metadata (timestamp, GPS/IP)

Status

“Client No-Show — Forfeited”

No manual review is required.

6. Successful Inspection Payment Logic

Conditions

All of the following must be true:

  • Client confirms completion

  • Agent submits valid booking code

  • Code is verified

  • Timer is valid

System Must:

  • Deduct 1 inspection credit

  • Mark inspection as certified

  • Allocate ₦2,000 to agent (withdrawable)

  • Allocate ₦500 to platform revenue

  • Lock session

  • Generate payout record

Status

“Completed — Certified”

7. Payment Distribution Engine

Distribution Rule

From every ₦2,500 payment:

Allocation Amount
Agent ₦2,000
Platform ₦500

Distribution Only Occurs When:

  • Inspection is certified, OR

  • Client no-show confirmed, OR

  • Late cancellation after lock

Early distribution is prohibited.
